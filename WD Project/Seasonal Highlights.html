Winter +

Best Places to Visit in Sweden During Winter

Abisko & Kiruna

Why? Best for Northern Lights hunting and unique experiences like staying at the ICEHOTEL. Abisko National Park boasts some of the clearest skies for viewing auroras. When? From December to March for ideal times of aurora sighting. What to Do? Aurora chasing, dog sledding, reindeer safaris, visiting the ICEHOTEL, and snowmobiling.

Stockholm

Why? Conjure up the enchanting beauty of the capital-with snow-clad streets, dripping history, and bustling Christmas markets. When? Late November to early January for holiday festivities or February for a quieter winter experience. What to Do? Ice skating, exploring Gamla Stan (Old Town), visiting museums, and enjoying Christmas markets.

Gothenburg

Why? Enchanting Christmas markets, especially at Liseberg Amusement Park, and cozy winter vibes. When? During the late part of November to December for the markets. What to Do? Go shop for some unique gifts, try fresh seafood, and explore the wintery archipelago.


Autumn +

Best Places to Visit in Sweden During Autumn

Stockholm Archipelago

Why? The islands are less crowded, and the foliage transforms into vibrant autumn colors, creating stunning scenery. When? September to October for peak fall colors. What to Do? Take a boat tour, hike scenic trails, or enjoy cozy fika in quaint seaside villages.

Abisko National Park

Why? Autumn in Abisko offers stunning golden birch forests and the first chances to see the Northern Lights as the skies darken. When? Late September to early October for fall foliage and aurora hunting. What to Do? Hike the Kungsleden Trail, enjoy photography, or try for early-season auroras.

Gothenburg

Why? A lively city with cultural events, harvest festivals, and cozy vibes during autumn. When? September to October for markets and seafood season. What to Do? Visit the Gothenburg Museum of Art, explore the botanical gardens, or enjoy fresh shellfish during the West Coast Shellfish Season.

Dalarna

Why? Known for its traditional Swedish countryside, Dalarna becomes a canvas of red, yellow, and orange during autumn. When? Late September to early October for the best foliage. What to Do? Explore Lake Siljan, visit historic villages, and enjoy outdoor activities like biking or kayaking.


Summer +

Best Places to Visit in Sweden During Summer

Stockholm Archipelago

Why? With over 30,000 islands, the archipelago is a summer paradise offering serene landscapes and outdoor activities. When? June to August for long sunny days and warm weather. What to Do? Go kayaking, take boat tours, enjoy picnics on secluded islands, or swim in the Baltic Sea.

Gotland

Why? Sweden’s largest island is famous for its medieval town of Visby (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), sandy beaches, and summer festivals. When? July for the Medieval Week Festival or August for fewer crowds. What to Do? Explore Visby’s cobblestone streets, relax on beaches, or hike along the island’s rugged coastline.

Malmö and Skåne (Southern Sweden)

Why? Known for its beaches, picturesque countryside, and vibrant city life. When? June to August for outdoor adventures and cultural events. What to Do? Visit the beaches of Falsterbo, cycle through the Skåne countryside, and enjoy Malmö’s parks and modern architecture.

Dalarna

Why? The heart of Swedish traditions, Dalarna is especially festive in summer with Midsummer celebrations. When? June for Midsummer (around June 21). What to Do? Participate in Midsummer festivities, hike around Lake Siljan, and visit traditional villages like Rättvik and Mora.

Gothenburg and the Bohuslän Coast

Why? Known for its seafood, charming fishing villages, and stunning coastline dotted with granite cliffs. When? June to August for the best weather. What to Do? Explore the Gothenburg archipelago, savor fresh seafood, and go island-hopping.


Spring +

Best Places to Visit in Sweden During Spring

Stockholm

Why? The city awakens with cherry blossoms, vibrant parks, and cultural events, offering a perfect blend of nature and city life. When? April to May for blooming flowers and milder weather. What to Do? Visit Kungsträdgården Park for cherry blossoms, explore the Skansen Open-Air Museum, and take boat tours through the city’s canals.

Malmö and Skåne

Why? Southern Sweden bursts into color with blooming tulips, vibrant rapeseed fields, and sunny weather. When? April to May for flower festivals and pleasant temperatures. What to Do? Walk through Malmö Castle Gardens, bike along the Skåne countryside, and visit the Sofiero Palace Gardens.

Lapland

Why? Spring offers a unique mix of snow-covered landscapes and emerging greenery, along with quiet trails. When? March to early May for snowy adventures and late May for the first signs of summer. What to Do? Go skiing, enjoy husky sled rides, or take peaceful hikes in Abisko National Park.

Gothenburg

Why? Known for its vibrant tulip displays and gardens, Gothenburg is a springtime delight for nature lovers. When? April to May for blooming flowers. What to Do? Stroll through Botaniska Trädgården (Botanical Garden), visit Liseberg Amusement Park, or enjoy seafood by the harbor.
